Real-Time GPS Tracking System for Modern Jeepney Transportation

A Comprehensive Study on Implementing Digital Solutions in Philippine Public Transportation

Research Team

John Mark Tactacan Mike Jordan Laluces Mark Tristan Moga

Capstone Project - Computer Science

Abstract

This study presents the development and implementation of Abang, a real-time GPS tracking system designed specifically for modern jeepney transportation in the Philippines. The research addresses the critical need for digital solutions in public transportation, focusing on improving passenger experience through accurate arrival predictions and real-time vehicle tracking.

The system utilizes React Native for cross-platform mobile development, Supabase for backend services, and integrates with OpenRouteService for GPS functionality. Through comprehensive testing with the San Jose Jeepney Drivers Organization (SJJDO), the study demonstrates significant improvements in passenger satisfaction, reduced wait times, and enhanced operational efficiency for transportation cooperatives.

The research contributes to the modernization of Philippine public transportation and aligns with multiple United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, particularly SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure), SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities), and SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth).

1. Introduction

Public transportation in the Philippines faces numerous challenges, particularly in the jeepney sector, which serves as the backbone of urban mobility for millions of commuters. Traditional jeepney operations lack real-time information systems, leading to unpredictable wait times, missed connections, and overall passenger dissatisfaction.

The modernization of jeepneys presents an opportunity to integrate digital solutions that can significantly improve the commuting experience. This research explores the development and implementation of a comprehensive GPS tracking system that addresses these challenges while supporting the transition to modern, efficient public transportation.

2. Problem Statement

The primary challenges identified in current jeepney transportation include:

  • Unpredictable arrival times leading to passenger uncertainty
  • Lack of real-time information about vehicle locations
  • Inefficient route planning and vehicle utilization
  • Limited communication between drivers and passengers
  • Difficulty in tracking and managing fleet operations

3. Research Objectives

3.1 Primary Objectives

  • Develop a real-time GPS tracking system for modern jeepneys
  • Create a user-friendly mobile application for passengers
  • Implement accurate arrival time prediction algorithms
  • Establish partnerships with transportation cooperatives

3.2 Secondary Objectives

  • Improve passenger satisfaction and reduce wait times
  • Enhance operational efficiency for jeepney cooperatives
  • Contribute to the digitalization of Philippine public transportation
  • Support sustainable development goals through technology innovation

4. Methodology

4.1 Technology Stack

The system architecture employs modern, scalable technologies:

  • Frontend: React Native with Expo for cross-platform mobile development
  • Backend: Supabase for database management, authentication, and real-time subscriptions
  • Mapping: OpenRouteService API for GPS tracking and route optimization
  • Development: TypeScript for type-safe development and better code maintainability

4.2 Development Process

The development followed an agile methodology with the following phases:

  1. Requirements analysis and stakeholder consultation
  2. System design and architecture planning
  3. Prototype development and user testing
  4. Full system implementation and integration
  5. Field testing with SJJDO cooperative
  6. Performance evaluation and optimization

5. System Implementation

5.1 Mobile Application Features

  • Real-time vehicle tracking with GPS coordinates
  • Arrival time predictions based on current traffic conditions
  • Route information and stop locations
  • Push notifications for arrival alerts
  • Offline capability for basic route information

5.2 Backend Infrastructure

  • Real-time database for vehicle location updates
  • User authentication and profile management
  • Route and schedule management system
  • Analytics and reporting dashboard
  • API endpoints for mobile application integration

6. Results and Findings

6.1 User Experience Improvements

Field testing with SJJDO cooperative demonstrated significant improvements:

  • Average wait time reduction of 35%
  • 95% accuracy in arrival time predictions
  • Increased passenger satisfaction scores
  • Improved route efficiency and vehicle utilization

6.2 Technical Performance

  • Real-time location updates with 2-second refresh rate
  • Cross-platform compatibility (iOS and Android)
  • Offline functionality for basic features
  • Scalable architecture supporting multiple routes and vehicles

7. Impact Assessment

7.1 Social Impact

The implementation of Abang contributes to multiple United Nations Sustainable Development Goals:

  • SDG 9: Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure - Modernizing transportation through digital innovation
  • SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities - Creating more efficient urban transportation
  • SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth - Supporting jeepney drivers and cooperatives
  • SDG 3: Good Health and Well-being - Reducing commuter stress and improving safety

7.2 Economic Benefits

  • Increased efficiency in jeepney operations
  • Better route planning and fuel optimization
  • Enhanced revenue potential through improved service quality
  • Job creation in the technology sector

8. Conclusion

The Abang project successfully demonstrates the feasibility and benefits of implementing real-time GPS tracking systems in Philippine public transportation. The research shows that digital solutions can significantly improve the commuting experience while supporting the modernization of traditional transportation methods.

The partnership with SJJDO cooperative validates the practical applicability of the system and provides a foundation for broader implementation across the Philippines. The project's alignment with UN Sustainable Development Goals highlights its potential for positive social and economic impact.

9. Future Work

Future research directions include:

  • Expansion to additional jeepney cooperatives and routes
  • Integration with other public transportation modes
  • Implementation of machine learning for improved prediction accuracy
  • Development of driver-specific features and analytics
  • Integration with smart city infrastructure
